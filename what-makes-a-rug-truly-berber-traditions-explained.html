Engaging a rug professional can ensure proper care, especially for long-pile wool rugs like Beni Ourain and Azilal rugs. Regularly vacuuming helps to keep dust away while protecting the intricate rug symbols—protection that reflect the culture and artistry of ancient Moroccans. This is true for any quality rug, from a traditional Berber to a modern photo rug. The classification of these rugs, such as zigzag-laden styles and other Moroccan examples, also dictates their specific care needs to uphold their beauty and longevity.

  • Regularly vacuum your Berber rug on a low setting to remove dirt and dust without damaging the fibers.
  • Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear and fading from sunlight.
  • Spot clean spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth to prevent staining.
  • Use a soft-bristle brush to gently lift dirt from the rug’s surface.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or cleaners; opt for wool-safe products.
  • When possible, take your rug outdoors and shake it out to rid it of dust and debris.
  • Have your rug professionally cleaned every few years to maintain its appearance and integrity.
